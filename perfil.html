Computational Investigation of Charge Transfer Pathways in Native 2Fe-2S Clusters: Insights from Density Functional Theory

The electron transport chain in eukaryotic cells plays a critical role in transforming glucose or photons into biologically available energy. Iron-sulfur (Fe-S) clusters, specifically the 2Fe-2S cluster, serve as crucial redox centers within this process. While research on the charge transfer dynamics of multiple iron-sulfur clusters has advanced, the study of single-cluster dynamics remains relatively unexplored. In this work, we employ a bottom-up approach to investigate the charge transfer properties of individual 2Fe-2S clusters using density functional theory (DFT) simulations. Our objective is to provide a foundation for the development of synthetic mimetics that can improve or replicate the function of these naturally occurring redox centers, with potential applications in organic photovoltaic solar cells and other bio-inspired technologies. We calculate the electron localization function (ELF) of 2Fe-2S clusters and conduct molecular orbital (MO) analysis of the highest occupied molecular orbital (HOMO) and lowest unoccupied molecular orbital (LUMO). By combining ELF and MO analyses, we identify and characterize possible charge transfer pathways, providing valuable insights into the factors that influence charge transfer efficiency in iron-sulfur clusters. Our findings suggest that nature has optimized the charge transfer properties of these molecular components, and further improvements may be achieved through targeted modifications, such as halogenation or heterometal substitution. This fundamental understanding of single-cluster charge transfer dynamics paves the way for the development of novel, efficient, and versatile bio-inspired materials and technologies.
